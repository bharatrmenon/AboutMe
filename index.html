The Journey to CEO

In 2016, I made a life-changing decision: I moved to the United States to pursue a master's degree in Innovation Management & Entrepreneurship at Brown University. My background was in engineering—I had spent years solving complex problems and publishing research in green energy at the National University of Singapore—but I wanted to learn how to transform technical ideas into scalable businesses.

When I met David Laughlin in 2017, we connected instantly. That meeting led to my first role at Pentagon Safety Equipment as a Business Operations Engineer. I quickly realized we were heavily reliant on wholesale customers—a risky model where losing one account could cripple sales. We were competing with industry giants on thin margins.

The turning point came in 2020. When COVID-19 hit, supply chain disruptions forced me to rethink everything. I realized we needed to own our destiny through e-commerce. I founded our digital division from scratch. The results were transformative: e-commerce sales skyrocketed from $10,000 in 2020 to over $1,000,000 by 2023. Today, e-commerce accounts for 90% of our total sales, a radical shift from the 2% it represented when I started.

That experience taught me the power of agility and data-driven decisions. Whether tackling a strategic challenge or untangling global logistics, I approach every obstacle with curiosity and a passion for building something sustainable.
