The Journey from Engineer to Entrepreneur

In 2016, I made a life-changing decision: I moved to the United States to pursue a master's degree in Innovation Management & Entrepreneurship at Brown University. My background was in engineering—I earned a master's degree in electrical engineering from the National University of Singapore and spent years solving complex problems and publishing research in green energy. But I wanted to go beyond technical expertise and deepen my understanding of business strategy.

In 2017, I joined Pentagon Safety Equipment as a Business Operations Engineer. At the time, we were heavily reliant on traditional wholesale—a risky model where losing even one major account could significantly impact sales. I saw untapped potential and a critical need for a strategic shift to e-commerce.

When the global pandemic hit in 2020, I founded and led our e-commerce division from scratch. The results were transformative: digital sales skyrocketed from $10,000 to over $1,000,000 within three years. Today, e-commerce accounts for 90% of our total sales. This journey from the ground floor to the CEO office taught me that sustainable growth requires the perfect blend of technical rigor and operational agility.
